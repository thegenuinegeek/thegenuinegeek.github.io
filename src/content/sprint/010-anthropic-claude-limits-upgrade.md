I need to be upfront about something. This article was written alongside Claude. Claude helped build this site. I use it every day for programming and data analysis at work. I'm a fan of the product.

And today, the product got me.

## The Feature Drop

March 27, 2026 was a big day for Anthropic. They shipped a stack of updates that, individually, would each be headline-worthy:

**[Sonnet 4.6](https://releasebot.io/updates/anthropic/claude)** launched as their most capable Sonnet model yet. Full upgrade across coding, computer use, long-context reasoning, agent planning, knowledge work, and design. 1M token context window in beta. This is the model powering the conversation you're reading right now.

**Claude Apps** brought interactive applications directly into chat. Charts, diagrams, shareable assets, all rendering inline on iOS and Android. It's the kind of feature that makes you rethink how you use a chat interface.

**Computer use agents** went live. [CNBC reported](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/24/anthropic-claude-ai-agent-use-computer-finish-tasks.html) that Claude can now operate your computer on your behalf while you're away. You tell it what to do, walk away, and it handles browser interactions, file management, and multi-step workflows autonomously.

**Cowork persistent threads** rolled out for Pro and Max plans. Tasks that keep running in the background. Set it, forget it, come back to results.

**Office integration upgrades** for Excel and PowerPoint now share full conversation context between apps. Build a chart in Excel, explain it in PowerPoint, and Claude remembers everything from both.

On top of all that, [Fortune broke the story](https://fortune.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-says-testing-mythos-powerful-new-ai-model-after-data-leak-reveals-its-existence-step-change-in-capabilities/) that an internal data leak revealed "Claude Mythos," described as a "step change" in AI capabilities. Anthropic confirmed the model exists and is in testing.

It's a lot. Genuinely impressive.

## The Other Announcement

Buried in the same news cycle, Anthropic also announced changes to session limits.

The short version: your 5-hour session limits now deplete faster during peak hours. Weekdays between 5am and 11am PT (1pm to 7pm GMT), you'll burn through your allocation quicker than before.

Weekly limits stay the same. But how they're distributed across the week is changing. Anthropic says about 7% of users will hit limits they wouldn't have previously, "particularly for pro tiers."

Their advice? Shift token-intensive work to off-peak hours.

## How I Found Out

I didn't find out from the announcement. I found out the hard way.

I was deep in a session today, using Claude heavily for work. Parsing data, generating analysis, iterating on outputs. Heavy but not unusual usage for me. Then I hit my 5-hour limit for the first time ever.

My immediate thought wasn't "they changed the limits." It was "wow, I'm using this more than I realized. Maybe I should upgrade."

So I did. I upgraded my plan on the spot.

It wasn't until later that I saw the announcement about the limit changes and realized what happened. I didn't hit the limit because I was using more. I hit the limit because the limit moved.

## The Chess Move

Here's the thing. I'm not mad. But I am impressed by the timing.

You ship the most compelling feature update in months. Sonnet 4.6 is genuinely better. Claude Apps is genuinely useful. Computer use is genuinely exciting. Every user is going to engage more heavily with these new capabilities.

Then, on the same day, you tighten the session limits during exactly the hours when professionals are using the product.

More engagement plus tighter limits equals more upgrades.

I'm one data point, but I'd bet good money I'm not the only person who upgraded today without realizing the limits had changed. The features made me use it more. The new limits made me hit the wall. The wall made me pay more.

That's not a bug. That's a funnel.

## The Bigger Picture

This fits a pattern we've seen across the AI industry. The free and low-cost tiers exist to build habit and dependency. Once you're integrated into your workflow, the pricing adjusts.

Anthropic isn't the only one doing this. OpenAI has adjusted ChatGPT Plus limits multiple times. Google's Gemini pricing has shifted. It's the standard SaaS playbook applied to AI.

The difference is that AI tools create a uniquely strong dependency. Once Claude is part of how you write, code, analyze, and think, switching costs are enormous. It's not like switching from Slack to Teams. It's like switching brains.

## The Genuine Geek Take

I want to be fair here. Anthropic was transparent about the change. They posted about it publicly. They gave specific numbers (7% of users affected). They explained the reasoning (demand management). They didn't hide it.

But they also timed it perfectly with a feature drop that guaranteed heavier usage. And for someone like me who uses Claude as a daily work tool, the result was a same-day upgrade without even knowing the rules had changed.

I'm still a fan. I'm still using it. The product is legitimately great and keeps getting better.

But I'm also now paying more for it. And I didn't know the rules had changed when I made that call.

Would've been nice to get the context about the changes to 5-hour limits in the message prompting me to upgrade. I probably still would've done it, but at least I would've been making an informed decision.
