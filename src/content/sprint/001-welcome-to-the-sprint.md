This domain used to be a landing page for a web design and social media management business I ran. That version of genuinegeekmedia.com had a contact form, a portfolio, and not much else. It served its purpose and then it sat there, collecting dust, waiting for me to figure out what it was supposed to be.

It took a while. But I think I figured it out.


## How This Started

There wasn't a single moment where I decided to start a blog. It was a slow build. I've always wanted some kind of outlet, a place to put the things I think about that don't fit neatly into a work Slack channel or a group text. Tech stuff. Gaming opinions. Car projects. The random intersection of all of it.

The thing that finally pushed me to actually do it was going on the [Great Playing With You](https://www.youtube.com/@greatplayingwithyou) podcast. Sitting down and talking about gaming, tech, and life with people who genuinely care about those things made me realize I had more to say than I thought. And that maybe it was worth saying it somewhere permanent.

So I rebuilt the site. Astro, Tailwind, a retro terminal aesthetic because I'm that kind of geek. And here we are.


## What Is The Sprint?

The Sprint is where I cover what's happening right now. News, takes, opinions. Gaming industry moves. Tech announcements. Whatever is on my mind that week.

It's not breaking news. I'm not a journalist. I'm not going to scoop anyone or have insider sources. I'm a dad with a full-time IT management job, two kids, a project car, and maybe an hour of free time after everyone goes to bed. My takes aren't the most informed. They're not the most timely. But they're mine.

That's the whole thing. This isn't a media company. It's one guy writing about the stuff he cares about in between bedtime routines and sprint reviews.


## Why "Genuine Geek"?

I've always felt a little out of place everywhere. Good at a lot of things but never wholly fixated on anything for long. I geek out on cloud infrastructure at work, then come home and geek out on LS engine builds in the garage, then put the kids to bed and geek out on whatever game I'm into that week.

There's no single community where all of that fits. Car guys don't care about my AWS opinions. Tech people don't want to hear about rocker arm trunion upgrades. Gamers don't need my take on third-gen F-body suspension geometry.

But all of it is genuinely me. Not performative. Not chasing trends. Not pretending to be deeper into any one thing than I actually am. Just a guy who is interested in a lot of stuff and wants to talk about it.

That's the "genuine" part. The "geek" part is self-explanatory.


## What You Can Expect

Honest takes from someone who doesn't have time to be diplomatic about them. Articles that sound like a conversation, not a press release. A perspective shaped by being a husband, a dad, a manager, a builder, and a gamer all at once.

The Sprint covers the news. [The Workbench](/workbench) goes deeper on builds and projects. [The Paddock](/paddock) is conversations with people doing interesting things.

I don't know how often I'll publish. I don't have an editorial calendar. I write when I have something to say and the kids are asleep.

Thanks for being here. Green flag drops now.
