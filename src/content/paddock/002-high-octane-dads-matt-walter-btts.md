This one goes out to anyone who's ever tried to explain to their spouse why they need another set of tires.

We sat down with Matt Walter on the High Octane Dads podcast leading up to the 2024 [Back to the Streets](https://backtothestreetsdnd.com/) event. Matt isn't just a competitor. He's a BTTS winner. And he had a lot to say about what it takes to show up, survive the drive, and actually be competitive at the end of it.

## What is Back to the Streets?

If you're not familiar with drag-and-drive events, the concept is simple and insane in equal measure.

You drive your car to a drag strip. You race. You drive your car to the next drag strip. You race again. Repeat for multiple days across multiple tracks.

No trailers. No tow rigs. The car you race is the car you drive between stops. If it breaks, you fix it on the side of the road or you're out.

[Back to the Streets](https://backtothestreetsdnd.com/) is a no-prep drag-and-drive event that's become one of the most exciting grassroots motorsport events in the country. It draws competitors across multiple classes including Small Tire, Stick Shift, Hard Tire, True Street, Modern Street, index classes, and even Diesel.

## The 2026 Event is Coming

BTTS 2026 is locked in for **May 20-24, 2026**. The route hits four tracks:

- **Bunker Hill Dragstrip** (tech and prep party)
- **Wabash Valley Dragway**
- **Wagler Motorsports Park**
- **Brown County Dragstrip**

Registration opened January 1st. If you've ever thought about entering a drag-and-drive event, this is the one.

## What We Talked About

Matt walked us through what goes into preparing for an event like this. It's not just about having a fast car. It's about having a reliable car that's fast. There's a big difference.

We got into the mental side of it too. You're hours from home, your car just made a hard pass, and now you need to drive it 100 miles to the next track in traffic. The trust you have to put in your build is real.

The family angle was the heart of the conversation. Matt's a dad. He races. Those two things don't always coexist easily. We talked about how he makes it work, how the racing community supports families, and how he's passing the passion down to the next generation.

## The Genuine Geek Take

Drag-and-drive events are the purest form of motorsport validation there is. Anybody can build a car that makes one pass on a prepped track. Building a car that races hard and then drives home? That's a different kind of engineering.

Matt's the real deal. Hearing him talk about the balance between competition and family resonated with me as a dad who spends his weekends in the garage. The car hobby is better when your family is part of it.
